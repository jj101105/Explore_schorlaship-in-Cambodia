Institute of Technology of Cambodia
(ITC)

Various organizations and institutions offer scholarships and financial aid to support students in Information Technology and Communication (ITC). Here are the main types of ITC scholarships available:

  • 1. Government ITC Scholarships: Offered by national governments to support students in IT fields, covering tuition and living expenses.
  • 2. International ITC Scholarships: Provided by global organizations or governments to encourage international students to study IT abroad.
  • 3. Merit-Based ITC Scholarships: Awarded to students based on academic achievements or technical expertise in IT fields.
  • 4. NGO & Private Sector ITC Scholarships: Offered by NGOs and private companies to support students, especially from underrepresented groups, in IT education.
  • 5. Research & Graduate ITC Scholarships: For graduate students and researchers pursuing advanced studies or research in IT fields like AI and cybersecurity.

Vision

ITC have plan to develop and enhance human resources with high potential in engineering, technology, science, and innovation. The Institute strives to become a leading institution in training young Cambodian students, equipping them with the skills and knowledge to excel. Additionally, ITC aims to establish itself as a renowned center for scientific and technological research, recognized both nationally and internationally.

Mission

ITC provide high-quality education and training in engineering, technology, and science. ITC is dedicated to equipping students with the knowledge, practical skills, and capabilities needed to meet the demands of the labor market, enabling them to contribute to job creation and Cambodia's socio-economic development.

Goals

To achieve its vision and objectives, the Institute has the following goals:

  • Develop and produce skilled, high-quality human resources that meet the needs of the labor market.
  • Continuously improve academic quality in engineering, technology, science, and innovation, while enhancing the higher education system in Cambodia.
  • Advance research efforts at both national and international levels.
  • Strengthen collaboration with local and international partners to enhance teaching, research, and the overall educational experience for students and faculty.
  • Foster strong relationships with industries, enterprises, and companies to create job opportunities and provide valuable study tours for students.

ITC exchange-program

The Institute of Technology of Cambodia (ITC) offers various exchange programs to enhance international academic collaboration and provide students with global exposure. Here are some key programs:

  • International Engineering Program: ITC offers a 5-year International Engineering Program leading to a master's-equivalent degree. This program is conducted in collaboration with partner universities in countries such as France, Australia, Malaysia, Thailand, Indonesia, and the European Union. Students have the opportunity to earn double degrees and participate in exchange programs with these international institutions. [Source]
  • Exchange Program with Kanazawa University, Japan: ITC has established an exchange program with Kanazawa University in Japan. This program allows ITC students to spend one semester studying at Kanazawa University, providing them with valuable international academic experience. [Source]
  • Erasmus+ Faculty Mobility: ITC participates in the Erasmus+ program, which supports faculty mobility for teaching and research purposes. Through this program, ITC faculty members have the opportunity to collaborate with European universities, enhancing academic exchange and development. [Source]
  • AUN/SEED-Net Collaboration: ITC collaborates with the ASEAN University Network Southeast Engineering Education Development Network (AUN/SEED-Net), facilitating student and faculty exchanges. This partnership aims to improve engineering education and foster regional cooperation. [Source]
  • Exchange Programs in Transport and Infrastructure Engineering: ITC's Transport and Infrastructure Engineering program offers exchange opportunities with partner universities in France, Thailand, and Japan. These exchanges provide students with international perspectives on transport and infrastructure development. [Source]
