Pannasastra University of Cambodia
(PUC)

PUC offers various scholarships and financial aid to support both local students. Here are the main types of scholarships available:

  • Undergraduate Scholarships: Offered to students from socioeconomically disadvantaged backgrounds, these scholarships provide financial support to undergraduate students who have completed at least one term at PUC. Eligible students must submit an application with required documents such as a family book or household registration document.
  • Graduate Scholarships - Master in International Human Rights Law: In collaboration with the Raoul Wallenberg Institute, PUC offers up to 15 scholarships for this program, including full scholarships and partial scholarships covering 50% of tuition fees. These scholarships are intended to support students pursuing graduate studies in human rights law.
  • External Scholarships: Various international scholarships are available to PUC students through platforms like WeMakeScholars. These scholarships cater to different fields of study and offer partial or full funding based on academic performance, leadership qualities, and eligibility criteria.

Scholarships

Pannasastra University of Cambodia (PUC) offers scholarships for students who meet specific criteria. These scholarships are available for both new and continuing students, and include various benefits depending on the student’s performance.

Eligibility Criteria for Scholarships

For New Candidates:

  • Must come from a family with a lower socio-economic background.
  • Have completed at least one term of study at PUC with a B average.
  • Must apply at the PUC Administration Office.
  • Have no history of disciplinary issues at PUC.
  • Maintain good attendance and academic honesty.
  • Full scholarship students must work at least 10 hours per week as student assistants.

For Continuing Candidates:

  • Must maintain at least a C average GPA each year.
  • Participate in the Work-Study Program (e.g., Office Assistant, Library Assistant).
  • Support university events as informed by officials.
  • Follow the Student Code of Conduct with model behavior and academic honesty.

Scholarship and Grade Policy

The scholarship award is based on the student’s GPA or grades. Students who perform poorly may have their scholarship reduced, suspended, or revoked.

GPA or Grade Scholarship Award
A, B, C Maintain the same award level
D 70% of the original scholarship
F 50% of the original scholarship
Drop out Lose scholarship completely

Options for Students with “F” or “D” Grades

For students who receive a "D" or "F" grade, there are options to improve their academic standing:

  • Option 1: Repeat the course with a partial payment (50% for “F”, 30% for “D”).
  • Option 2: Take a “Remedial” English course for $55 to improve proficiency.

Scholarship Recipients and Class Schedule Policy

Scholarship recipients must adhere to specific class schedules based on their academic year and work status:

  • Foundation Year to 2nd Year: Full scholarship students can only attend morning or afternoon classes.
  • 3rd Year and Beyond: Non-working students cannot take evening classes. Working students may request evening classes with documentation of employment.

Scholarship renewal applications must be submitted by October 31 of each academic year. Failure to apply may result in loss of scholarship eligibility.
